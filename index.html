No. 02 · About

Named for the production company Kendo's father never opened.

Kendo Naken's father ran a Bangkok production house in the 90s. He nearly called it Akirart — but worried the name would be hard to pronounce, so he kept the company as Kamarart instead. Twenty-something years later, his son and a friend, New Athinan, took over the old third-floor office in a Plubplachai shophouse. They left the floppy disks where they were. They added a coffee machine.

"Walking into Akirart felt like going into a time capsule that brought me back to the era of 'chubby' computers and floppy disks." — TheSmartLocal · Eddie Jirayu · Dec 2019

The cafe opened in February 2019. A year later they moved to a bigger room at 138 Soi Pradu, in Pom Prap Sattru Phai — the corner of Chinatown where Bangkok's printing-house alleys used to live. The vintage Macs migrated with them. So did the steel filing cabinets, the typewriters, the photo booth, and a glass-walled bay labelled Film Photography Service Processing & Scanning — a working film lab that develops your 35mm and 120 negatives while you finish your Passion Fruit Espresso.

The drinks are good. The dad's name is on the door. The floppy disks come out as coasters under your cup.

No. 05 · Film lab

A real film-processing & scanning lab.

The glass-walled upstairs bay isn't a prop. Akirart processes 35mm and 120 negatives, hands the scans back as files, and exhibits the prints on the wall. Drop your rolls at the bar.

Formats accepted
35mm colour negative · 35mm B&W · 120 medium format. Drop-off at the bar.
Service
Processing only · Processing + scanning · scanning only. Scans delivered via Google Drive link.
Turnaround
Typically 3–5 days · longer during festivals and weekends.
Drop-off hours
Wed–Mon 10:00 – 17:30 · closed Tuesdays · DM @akirart.coffee first if you're bringing multiple rolls.
Price guide
Ask the bar — varies by film stock and scan resolution. Cheaper than a chain lab, slower than mail-order.
